Great Horned Owl

Bubo virginianus
  • They are found throughout North America and are equally at home in wetlands, forests, deserts, prairies, and cities.
  • They a are fierce predators, taking prey as large as osprey and other owls.
  • When they clench their talons, it takes a force of 28 lbs to get them open. This grip is used to sever the spines of their prey.
  • The female is larger than the male, but the male's voice is deeper than the female's.
